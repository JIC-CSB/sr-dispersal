Risk model of Stem Rust Aeciospore Dispersal

How is the SR aeciospore dispersal modelled?

A Gaussian Plume Model (GPM) was used here to determine how far away aeciospores could travel from a barberry bush. This information can then be used to plant barberry bushes far away enough from wheat fields.

What are the key factors in the GPM?

  1. Source Strength. How many aeciospores are being released from the barberry bush
  2. Shape of the Plume. How the 'cloud of aeciospores' looks like.

What affects the shape of the plume?

Environmental conditions such as:
  • Irradiance from the sun
  • Percentage of cloud cover
  • Wind speed

    • How do these factors affect the shape of the plume?

    The plume can be:

    1. Unstable. When wind speed is low and Irradiance is high (summer conditions).
    2. Neutral. When it is cloudy and wind speed is high.
    3. Stable. When the sky is clear and wind speed is low (at night - no solar radiation).
